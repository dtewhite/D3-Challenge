Correlations Discovered Between Health Care Coverage and Poverty

What impact does poverty have on health care coverage? This project uses state data from the census to try to answer that questions. The above graph shows the percent of people in every state that have health insurance and the percent of people in every state that live below the poverty line.

It is not too surprising that there appears to be a positive linear relationship between how much poverty is in each state and how many people do not have health insurance. You can imagine that someone living in poverty will may not have a job that provides health insurance and may not be able to afford private coverage either.
